Developing High Throughput (Stem) Cell Arrays for Studying Cell Properties and Behavior

Stem cells represent a resource of vast potential, but the complex biology that governs their behavior is often difficult to study and manipulate. We of the Clark Lab "Dark Side" are seeking to change this by developing next generation high-throughput screening modalities and native reporter systems. My work specifically focuses on the throughput half of the equation, seeking to extend the non-contact-printed custom chip systems used for toxicity screening in the lab's past to questions within the wider space of stem cell biology.
