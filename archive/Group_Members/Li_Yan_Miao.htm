Research Interests: Gene Expression of Extremophiles

My research involves studying the genomic expression profile of Methanococcus jannaschii at different temperatures. M. jannaschii is an extremophile that grows under high temperature and pressure. Under heat shock stress, certain protein(s) is expressed/induced in order to help it survive. These include small heat shock protein (sHSP) and chapreonins. These proteins can refold the non-functional proteins into the proper conformation, enabling M. jannaschii cells to survive longer under heat shock stress. By analyzing the genomic expression profile of M. jannaschii under temperature stress, we should be able to obtain some useful information for stress proteins that can increase the production of recombinant proteins from extremophiles cloned into mesophiles such as Escherichia coli.

We are using DNA microarray technology to understand gene expression profiles in M. jannaschii grown under stress and northern blots to confirm array results. My work also involves some RNA extraction and operating the high pressure-temperature reactor used for cultivating M. jannaschii that is located in the Clark lab.
