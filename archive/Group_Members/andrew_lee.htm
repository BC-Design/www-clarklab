Research Interests: Solid Phase Combinatorial Biocatalysis of Indinavir

The overall goal of this project is to develop the methods to use combinatorial biocatalysis to derivatize solid- and polymer-supported lead compounds.  This is an important research area because it has wide implications on drug discovery.  We must demonstrate that enzymatic catalysis can be performed on solid- and polymer-supported molecules like bergenin and indinavir by developing tools and methods to carry out such reactions.  To determine the effectiveness of these methods, it is important to analyze the kinetics of the reactions: my current role in this project.

Kinetic data is one of the concrete pillars for this research.  Determining the reactivity of compounds is the standard by which different reaction systems are compared.  Kinetic data gives undeniable quantified numbers that allows for quick comparison among similar reactions.  One key point we want to analyze in this project is to compare how our solid-phase reactions fair with the similar solution phase reactions.  By determining the kinetics of various bergenin-derivitization reactions in solution such as acylations, halogenations, and hydroxylations, we begin to form the basis for this comparison.

In addition to obtaining kinetic data to compare different reaction systems, we use it to compare the reactivity of a compound in different types of reaction media.  For example, we can compare the reactivity in purely organic solvents with the same reaction in organic and aqueous mixtures.  By doing so, we can determine the optimal reaction conditions in which to run our reactions.  These optimizations are one of the key tasks in chemical engineering.
