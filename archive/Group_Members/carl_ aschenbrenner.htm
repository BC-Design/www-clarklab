Research Interests: Solid Phase Combinatorial Biocatalysis of Indinavir

The overall goal of this project is to develop solid-phase combinatorial biocatalysis as a new drug discovery tool. In order to do this, it will be necessary to attach, derivatize, and cleave solid- and polymer-supported lead compounds.  My initial role will be to:

1)      Employ solubilized enyzmes to derivatize the natural flavonoid bergenin in an organic solution. Normally, enzymes are insoluble in organic solvents. Therefore, in order to carry out organic-phase derivatization reactions on a solid-supported substrate, it is necessary to use solubilized enzymes. The solubilization procedure involves contacting an aqueous buffer solution containing the enzyme with an anionic surfactant dissolved in an organic solvent.

2)      Obtain kinetic data for the acylation of bergenin, in particular Km and Vmax. These results will then be used as a baseline with which to compare the reactivity of the solid-attached substrate.
