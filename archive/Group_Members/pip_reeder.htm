Research Interests: High-Throughput Screening

The aim of this research is to develop a high-throughput screening technique for cyto-toxic and cyto-static drug therapies. Therapeutic enzymes of interest are encapsulated and expressed in a gel matrix which is dropped onto a glass plate capable of taking thousands of such drops (~5uL each). Cells are then deposited onto these drops and allowed to grow or die in the presence the drug. Visualization with a live/dead assay yields a quantification of activity of the drugs screened. Theoretically, this technology will enable screening of hundreds of therapeutic analogs in series on one small microscope slide.
