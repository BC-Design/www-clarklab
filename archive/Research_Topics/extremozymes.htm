Structure and Function of Extremozymes:

Complementary to our research on extremophiles is our continuing investigation of enzyme structure-function relationships under rigorous conditions of practical interest, for example, at high temperatures and pressures. For example, we have found that that many thermophilic enzymes are substantially stabilized at high temperatures by high pressure. This effect appears to correlate with the internal hydrophobicity and/or the structural rigidity of the protein. High-pressure studies have thus helped to elucidate the importance of hydrophobic interactions and internal flexibility in protein stabilization. Significant activation by pressure has also been observed for some thermophilic proteins.
