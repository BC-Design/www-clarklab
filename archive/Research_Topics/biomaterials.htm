Biomaterials and Biocatalytic Microsystems:

A recent research activity of the Clark Group is the microfabrication of sol-gel structures containing active enzymes for microfluidic biocatalysis. This work is providing a foundation for the microfabrication of functional protein chips using sol-gel processes, and in related research, we have developed a reusable enzyme array for high-throughput biosynthesis and screening. The central design of the solzyme array can also be applied in the development of microanalytical, sensing, and diagnostic devices.

The Clark Group and collaborators have also developed a new methodology for preparing films and paints for use as biocatalytic coatings. This work has produced the first report of a biocatalytic paint suitable for coating a variety of surfaces. Biocatalytic coatings and paints have potential applications across many sectors of biotechnology, for example, as anti-fouling treatments for medical devices and implants, as fouling-resistant membranes, and as biocatalysts for industrial reactions.
