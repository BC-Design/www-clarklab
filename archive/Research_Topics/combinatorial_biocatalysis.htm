Combinatorial Biocatalysis:

The Clark Group and co-workers have also pioneered the development of combinatorial biocatalysis, a new technology in which enzymatic reactions and microbial transformations are used to generate libraries of new derivatives from an existing lead compound. Reactions are performed iteratively (as pictured below); new derivatives of the original lead compound are produced through multiple iterations of biocatalytic reactions. This methodology is now being used for the synthesis of potential pharmaceuticals and other active compounds, e.g., new analogs of the anticancer drugs taxol and doxorubicin. Current efforts are focused on the generation of new triterpenoids that may function as anti-cholesterol agents, starting from unique natural products of a deep-sea organism, and on the biocatalytic synthesis of new compounds active against breast cancer.
